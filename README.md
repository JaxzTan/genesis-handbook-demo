# Genesis

**An Annual Hackathon Handbook, Written by the Community, for the Community.**

No single voice. No outdated guides. Just builders, helping builders.

---

## The Problem

The knowledge exists. It's just nowhere.

Newcomers show up to hackathons with zero direction. They have so many questions, but hackathons are too fast to allow that. Most existing guides are outdated, lacking depth, or too academic — you get the *what*, but never the actual *how* and *why*.

The community has the knowledge, but it's buried in a thousand random conversations with seniors, mentors, and Discord groups. We're missing a resource that covers the messy hackathon reality — everything from scrounging for a team on Discord at 2:00 AM to surviving the "demo-day curse" when your front-end suddenly stops talking to the blockchain.

## What Genesis Is

One living, community-vetted handbook.

Instead of a single voice attempting to cover everything, we crowdsource expertise — each contributor documenting exactly what they know best. The result is a resource more comprehensive, balanced, and current than any individual could ever produce. Updated annually. Forged from real hackathon experience. Entirely owned by the community.

- **Annual** — always current, never left to go stale
- **Many voices** — not one person trying to cover everything
- **3 phases** — structured contribution leading to a unified voice

## How It's Written

Each year, contributions open to anyone with hackathon experience. Together, they write the handbook through three structured phases.

1. **Map It** — Contributors propose topics they believe belong in the guide. Everything is on the table.
2. **Describe It** — Contributors write descriptions for each topic compiled from Phase 1.
3. **Advise It** — For each topic, contributors selectively add their tips, insights, and real-world advice.

Each phase runs with 3–5 days between them to consolidate. After each phase, submissions are reviewed and synthesized by AI into a single, balanced unified voice — preserving depth while removing redundancy.

## What Gets Covered

Topics evolve each year based on what contributors surface. Year 1 — shaped by real experience, not assumptions.

- What a hackathon is and how it's structured
- Judging criteria and how to think like a judge
- Team management and defining roles
- From idea to pitch
- Tech stacks by role
- Web3-specific tracks, tooling, and culture

## Roadmap

**Year 1 — Lay the Foundation**
- 20–30 contributors for the inaugural edition
- Publish the first edition of Genesis
- Promote through hackathon communities to grow the contributor base
- Bring in developers to maintain and build the platform

**Year 2 & beyond — Grow the Community**
- Story feed for contributors and participants to share experiences
- Voting system introduced across all three phases
- Platform goes open source
